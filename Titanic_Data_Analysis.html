Divide and conquer¶

From wikipedia, the real Titanic had passengers of 2224. So this example data is only part of the whole data, with a survival rate similar to the historical number. There are two approaches to analyze the data:

  1. calculate the correlation between one factor (Pclss, Age, Sex, Fare) and survival
  2. plot the distribution of survival in terms of one factor

0 general¶

Some quick ideas can be drawn from below:

  1. upper class has larger survival rate
  2. yonger people has slightly larger surival rate
  3. more expensive fare has larger survival rate

Summary¶

Conclusions¶

  1. Babies and women have much higher survival rate than other group.
  2. Upper class passengers have much higher survival rate than other group.

Limitations¶

  1. It seems groupby data doesn't have much plot methods. I still need to explore more to present better visualization.
  2. The conclusions are not surprising, so it doesn't provide much insights. In contrary, insights can be gained by study the fatal rate. In hindsight, we know the insufficient lifeboat is a key factor that dominate the survival rate.
